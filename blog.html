Outputs / Learnings

1. Understanding of Career Paths in Computing Learning Outcome: Students will develop a broad understanding of the various career paths within the computing profession, including software development, network administration, cybersecurity, data science, AI/ML, IT consulting, and more. They will learn how each of these fields contributes to different industries and the skills required to succeed in each role. Output: A comprehensive career roadmap or guide, detailing the different career options, required qualifications, industry trends, and potential career growth in the computing field.

2. Knowledge of Key Skills and Competencies Learning Outcome: Students will gain insight into the key technical and soft skills that are essential for success in the IT industry. These include problem-solving, communication, teamwork, programming languages, database management, and cloud technologies, as well as ethical decision-making and professional conduct. Output: A personal skills assessment or portfolio that outlines the skills the student has developed or needs to work on, along with strategies for continuous professional development.

3. Awareness of Industry Standards and Ethical Practices Learning Outcome: Students will be introduced to the ethical and legal challenges faced by computing professionals, such as data privacy, intellectual property, cybersecurity, and professional integrity. They will understand the importance of adhering to industry standards, legal regulations, and ethical guidelines. Output: A case study analysis or written reflection that discusses a real-world ethical dilemma in computing and proposes potential solutions based on professional and ethical standards.

4. Understanding of Professional Development and Certifications Learning Outcome: Students will explore the importance of lifelong learning in the computing profession, including professional certifications, continuing education, and attending industry events and conferences. They will gain knowledge about various industry-recognized certifications (e.g., CompTIA, Cisco, AWS, Microsoft).
